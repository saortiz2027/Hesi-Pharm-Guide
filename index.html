Exam Overview & Important Details

Course: NURS 3100 Pathophysiology and Pharmacology II

Semester: Spring 2026

Exam Weight: 15% of overall course grade

Category: Standalone gradebook category

Reference: BSN Handbook

This is not 5%, not 10%, and not 13%. This is 15% of your overall course grade. The HESI Pharmacology exam has its own category in the gradebook, which means it carries real weight. Preparing for it and taking it seriously truly matters.

At the same time, not passing this exam does not define your intelligence, your effort, or your worth as a future nurse. It is a standardized tool the school uses to evaluate readiness and predict NCLEX success. It measures performance on a specific day under specific conditions, not your potential.

Even though the exam exists for program evaluation, it still counts toward your grade. That reality is important to acknowledge. This is not meant to create fear, but focus. You have the ability to prepare, to improve, and to show growth. Approach this exam as an opportunity to strengthen your foundation, sharpen your clinical thinking, and move one step closer to where you are going.

This moment is about progress, not perfection. You are capable of more than you think.

A Message to the May 2027 Cohort

Click here to read the message

Dear May 2027 Cohort,

It is easy to wonder why studying now matters when the semester has not even begun. The truth is that starting early is not about doing more than everyone else. It is about giving yourself space.

When you prepare ahead of time, learning happens with clarity instead of urgency. Concepts settle in rather than being forced through panic. Stress stays lower, confidence grows faster, and you avoid being pushed into constant catch-up mode.

Nursing school favors consistency over cramming. Early studying turns exams into familiar check-ins instead of emergencies. It gives you flexibility when life happens and allows you to walk into the semester already recognizing the material, not meeting it for the first time.

I write this as a reminder to you, and to myself, that we are allowed to make things easier on ourselves. This is also a message we can return to and build together. Every small step taken early is an act of care for your future self and the nurse you are becoming. You do not have to have everything figured out. You just have to keep showing up.

With love,
